Reflective Design

This section explores how the site supports deeper meaning, long term user value, and identity expression.

Reflective design engages the user and their sense of belonging, going beyond immediate usability and aesthetics.

Value Mapping

The design reflects core values of community, creativity, and contribution. By aligning UI tone and structure with these values, the user feels their input matters.

Imagery and Tone

  • Visual elements are warm and go well together. The published images from the users will contribute to a sense of community and belonging. The design is inviting and encourages users to share their stories and experiences.
  • All images must include descriptive alt text to support accessibility for screen reader users.
  • Icons follow a minimalist, outlined style and are consistent throughout the site.

Language and Expression

Friendly, inclusive, and empowering language is used across calls-to-action, buttons, and headings. The tone aligns with a human-centered design philosophy. The websiteâ€™s tone is friendly, inclusive, and supportive. Content speaks directly to the user, with a conversational but respectful tone. Writing is clear, jargon-free, and avoids overly technical language, aiming to engage users of all ages and backgrounds.

Seeing Ourselves

Community contributions are highlighted to help users feel represented. This includes showing submitted names, photos, and messages (with consent).
